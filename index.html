What am I doing?

One of my biggest interests in programming is machine learning. I cannot pinpoint the origin of this, but I remember trying (and failing horribly) to create AIs on Python as an eleven year old. In 2019, I combined this with my interest in evolution to create my simulation of evolution by natural selection. This project involved simulating simple creatures in a simple environment containing only others of themselves and some food. The creatures had the ability to mate with another of the opposite sex to produce offspring with mutated "genes", and this project produced some interesting results which you can see here:

The creatures also had an energy cost which was subtracted from their total energy over time (or from reproduction) and could only be replenished with food. As seen from the graphs, these factors provided enough incentive for the population's traits to evolve over time. While this isn't strictly machine learning, it ties into neuroevolution which is what I am using for my current project which I am very excited about.

Another of my computing loves has been procedural generation. This is because when Minecraft came out, as a child I was awed by the endless possibilities of its world generation and by the fact it can create enormous terrains randomly yet without being totally chaotic. After years of occasional attempts to recreate this effect, I recently managed to achieve a simple version of this using Perlin Noise in my entry for October 2019's Ludum Dare game jam in the procedural generation of 2D deserts.
