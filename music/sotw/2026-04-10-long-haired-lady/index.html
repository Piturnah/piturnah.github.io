Song of the Week

(2026-04-10)

Long Haired Lady

by Paul & Linda McCartney

Song of the week! This is a project which my friends Anna and Sof have been doing since forever1 and which I’ve repeatedly said I would join in on, but never actually started it — until now! The catalyst for starting my own SotW has been finally getting around to ditching Spotify,2 as I’m now self-hosting a navidrome server for me and my partner.3 Setting up the infrastructure around this — such as last.fm for scrobbling stats — has led to me spending a lot more time than usual thinking about music; and I decided I wanted to carve out a little space on this site to talk about it. So, here we are!

Our first SotW comes from RAM, the debut album of Paul & Linda McCartney as musical collaborators. I’ve always been a huge fan of the Beatles — particularly of George Harrison — but I had not looked particulary into Paul’s solo work until my friend Amy recommended I check this album out. It’s since become one of my favourite albums and has been playing almost on repeat. The challenge for me this week was choosing which RAM song to feature here.

The first tracks that come to mind as standouts are probably Uncle Albert / Admiral Halsey and Heart of the Country (for some reason I think you especially should listen to the latter one if you’re reading this, Anna). Uncle Albert is an absolute classic and the only track I knew prior to looking at the full album. In the end I settled on Long Haired Lady, which — based on last.fm scrobbles — seems to be a bit of an underrated gem.4 This song is very representative of the pop whimsy which defines much of Paul’s writing and especially RAM. The vocal delivery feels beautifully tongue-in-cheek, especially with Linda’s “do you love me like you know you ought to do?” It’s a feel-good song on a feel-good album. Love is an explicit topic on a few of the tracks, but even when it’s not the whole thing feels like an earnest love letter to Paul & Linda’s relationship — which just puts me in a great mood every time. One of the hallmarks of my favourite McCartney songs is a sense that we’ve married a handful of fairly distinct tracks together, such as in Band on the Run or Uncle Albert / Admiral Halsey, and this charming feature is too present on Long Haired Lady.

Writing about music is difficult and distracting from the fact that you should just go and listen to it. The whole of Ram, that is. I’ve only mentioned a few of the tracks by name but the whole thing is truly special. It manages to somehow remain consistent while also exploring a surprising range of influences; certain tracks feel quite inspired by the Beach Boys, such as The Back Seat of my Car, whilst another of my favourite tracks is the nonsensical Monkberry Moon Delight5 — with its incredibly distinctive vocal style attributed by McCartney to shock rock artist Screamin’ Jay Hawkins.

See you next Friday. Hopefully.
